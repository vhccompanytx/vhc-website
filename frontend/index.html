FAQ

Your Questions, Answered in Plain English

How do you mount panels without damaging my roof or causing leaks?
We use professional-grade flashings and roof-specific mounting hardware designed to interlock directly with your structural rafters. Every single penetration point is triple-sealed with industrial roofing sealant to ensure absolute waterproofing that will outlast the roof itself. Our installations strictly follow Texas building codes to guarantee your roof's integrity.
How fast do solar panels lose their efficiency over time?
Modern Tier-1 monocrystalline panels degrade at a minimal rate of around 0.3% to 0.4% per year, even under extreme Texas heat exposure. This means that after 25 years, your system is guaranteed to still produce at least 85% of its original rated capacity. We exclusively install premium panels backed by bankable 25-year performance warranties.
How long does the whole installation process take?
The actual installation typically takes 1-2 days. The full project. Including permits, utility approval, and final inspection. Usually runs 4 to 8 weeks depending on your city and utility provider. VHC manages everything, so you don't have to track a single step.
Will my solar panels keep working when the power goes out?
A standard solar system without a battery is required to shut off automatically when the grid goes down. It's a safety requirement from Texas utility code. If you want to stay powered through an outage, you need a battery backup system added to your installation. Your solar panels charge the battery during the day, and the battery keeps your home running even if the grid is down for days at a time.
Can I run an EV charger directly off my solar panels?
Yes. When the sun is shining, the power generated by your panels prioritizes your home's immediate needs. Including your EV charger. This effectively allows you to charge your vehicle for free using pure sunshine. We also ensure your electrical panel has the correct capacity to handle the heavy draw of a Level 2 charger safely.
